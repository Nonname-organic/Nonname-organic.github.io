**Serial Imaging-based Deep Learning Effectively Predicts Survival in Immunotherapy-treated NSCLC**
=====================================================

**導入**
=====================================================

Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) is one of the most common types of lung cancer, and immunotherapy has become a promising treatment option in recent years. However, the effectiveness of immunotherapy can vary greatly depending on the individual patient. In a recent study published in Hospital Healthcare Europe, researchers have developed a deep learning model that uses serial imaging data to predict the survival rate of patients with NSCLC treated with immunotherapy.

**ニュースの背景・問題点**
=====================================================

The study, led by researchers at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), aimed to develop a predictive model that could accurately identify patients who would benefit from immunotherapy treatment. The team used a dataset of 1,200 patients with NSCLC who received immunotherapy treatment and had serial imaging data available. The dataset included computed tomography (CT) scans and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans taken at various time points during the treatment.

The researchers found that the deep learning model was able to predict the survival rate of patients with NSCLC treated with immunotherapy with high accuracy. The model was able to identify patients who were more likely to respond to immunotherapy treatment and those who were at higher risk of developing resistance to the treatment.

**現状の影響**
=====================================================

The development of a predictive model that can accurately identify patients who will benefit from immunotherapy treatment has significant implications for the treatment of NSCLC. Currently, immunotherapy treatment is often used as a last resort for patients who have exhausted other treatment options. However, with the ability to accurately predict which patients will respond to immunotherapy treatment, doctors may be able to use this treatment earlier in the treatment process, potentially improving patient outcomes.

In addition, the predictive model could also be used to identify patients who are at higher risk of developing resistance to immunotherapy treatment. This could allow doctors to adjust the treatment plan accordingly, potentially improving patient outcomes.
